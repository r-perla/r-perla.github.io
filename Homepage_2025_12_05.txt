a) Research overview [Darstellung abgeschlossener, laufender und geplanter Forschung]
Main research questions and methods
My overall research questions are:
1) Which neuro-cognitive mechanisms underlie social learning and decision-making? 
2) How do humans succeed or fail to achieve cooperation? 
3) Why do patients with psychiatric disorders (in particular with Borderline Personality Disorder, BPD) have difficulties maintaining mutually supportive social interactions? 
To address these questions, my published, ongoing, and future studies often use and combine: 
- computational modeling of behavioral data
- simulations using reinforcement learning, evolutionary game theory, and deep neural networks
- model-based and multivariate analyses of functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI)
- pupillometric and virtual reality data
Members of my group and I develop novel paradigms and apply state-of-the-art modelling techniques. The findings from our current projects highlight important open questions about the neuro-cognitive mechanisms of social learning and decision-making in healthy individuals and in patients with psychiatric disorders. I will submit several focused applications for individual research grants (e.g., through the DFG) to address these questions.
My research is guided by Marr’s tripartite framework: First, for developing novel paradigms, I clearly specify the goals, problems, and situations that individuals with or without social problems face. Second, I derive computational models to mathematically characterize behavioral strategies. Third, I apply fMRI (and pupillometry) to characterize the neural implementation.
